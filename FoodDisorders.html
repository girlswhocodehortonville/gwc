Food Disorders

The most common eating disorders are:

      Anorexia nervosa

    Anorexia nervosa is an eating disorder characterized by self-starvation, unrealistic fear of weight gain, and conspicuous distortion of body image.

      Bulimia nervosa

    Bulimia nervosa is an eating disorder characterized by overeating, followed by purging of the food in some way.

      Avoid and restrictive food intake disorder

    Avoid and restrictive food intake disorder is a type of eating disorder where the consumption of certain foods is limited based on the food's appearance, smell, taste, texture, or a past negative experience with the food.

Some of the most common treatments are:

      Family-based therapy
      Enhanced Cognative behavioral therapy
      Interpersonal therapy
      Cognative remediation therapty
      Unidentified protocol for transdiagnosic treatment of emotional disorders
