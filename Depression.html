Facts about depression: About 2 million Americans deal with depression. We know it's hard to deal with and share your feelings. Being sad is not the only thing to depression. If you think you have depression, go through these simple questions; Are you getting frustrated and mad over random things that you usually don't get mad or frustrated at? Are you having any of these symptoms?:

  • Depressed mood
  • Loss of pleasure in all or most activities
  • Significant weight change or change in appetite
  • Change in sleep
  • Change in activity
  • Fatigue or loss of energy
  • Diminished concentration
  • Feeling of guilt or worthlessnes

Depression has different triggers, it can be genetic or caused by a certain event. Depression even causes problems to the body, headaches, stomache problems, shortness of breath, and general physical tension. Depression can be a gut feeling. A depressed brain looks different than an undepressed brain. A person might not look depressed and they might say they're fine when really they aren't. Exercise can help mannage depression, however therepy is needed and antidepressants may be needed too. Being warmer can help. Happy things can lead to depression including graduation, or getting married. You need to remember depression is a disability and it requires treatment to help get better.
