Music and Depression

Listening to music may soothe many people. It is used in therapy sometimes even. Music therapy allows patients to boost their mood and relax. Playing instruments helps some people but many get frustrated. Studies have been carried out to prove music is a “treatment” for kids, teens and adults with depression. Those studies show that music is not just for fun but can also be used to raise self-esteem. A growing movement of music therapists and psychologists who are investigating the use of music in medicine to help patients dealing with pain, depression and possibly even Alzheimer's disease have found this to be true.
